Focaccia

Image by freepik

Description

This heavenly-smelling bread is soft and chewy on the inside with a crisp, cruchy exterior. For the best flavor and texture, let the dough rest in the fridge overnight.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups warm water 100-110°F
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 4 1/2-5 cups all purpose flour or bread flour plus more for hands

Topping and Pan

  • 5 tablespoons extra virgin oilve oil
  • 2 garlic cloves minced
  • 3-4 tablespoons chopped fresh basil, thyme, and rosemary
  • sprinkle of salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Use a spatula to mix together 1 cup of the warm water, 2 teaspoons sugar, and 2 teaspoons yeast together in a bowl. Cover and let dough rest for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the remaining water, oliver oil, salt, and 1 cup flour. Beat for 20 seconds, then add 3 1/2 cups more flour. Beat for 2 minutes. If the dough is still sticking to the sides of the bowl, add the last 1/2 cup of flour.
  3. Knead dough for 5 minutes on lightly floured surface. If the dough becomes too sticky durning kneading, sprinkle 1 tablespoon of flour at a time on dough. Do NOT add more flour than needed, you don't want a dry dough.
  4. The dough should still feel a little soft when kneading is done. Poke dough with your finger; if it bounces back, it's ready to rise. You can also Tear off a golfball sized piece from your dough, stretch it until light passes through, and hold it up to a window. If the light passes through the dough without it tearing it's ready to rise. If not, keep kneading until it passes this test.
  5. Lightly grease a large bowl with a tablespoon of oil or nonstick spray. You can use the same bowl that was used for the dough. Place the dough in the bowl and turn it to coat all side with the oil. Cover bowl with aliminum foil, plastic wrap, or a clean kitchen towel. Allow the dough to rise at room temperature for 2-3 hours or until double in size.
  6. Generously grease a 12x17-inch baking pan with 2 tablespoons of olive oil. This is the base layer of the bread.
  7. When the dough is ready, puch it down to release air bubbles. Place in the oiled baking pan, then stretch and flatten the dough to fit the pan. Don't tear the dough. If your dough is shrinking, cover with a clean towel and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before continuing.
  8. Cover the dough tightly and let it rest in the fridge for at least 1 hour and up to 24 hours. The longer it rests the better the flavor. At least 12 hours (overnight) is recommended.
  9. Remove dough from fridge and let sit at room temperature while you preheat the oven and prepare your toppings. Keep it covered.
  10. Preheat oven to 450°F and allow to heat for at least 10-15 minutes.
  11. Whisk 3 remaining tablespoons of olive oil with minced gralic, rosemary, and thyme. Set aside.
  12. Using your fingers, dimple the dough all over the surface. Drizzle the olive oil topping and use your hands or a patry brush to spread all over the top. Add a little more olive oil if needed so the dough is completely covered. Sprinkle with coarse salt and ground black pepper.
  13. Bake for 20-23 minutes or until lightly browned on top. If desired, broil on high for last minute to really brown top.
  14. Cut and serve. Enjoy!
